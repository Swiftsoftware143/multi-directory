📦 Supplier Guide

Everything you need to know about registering as a supplier, managing your catalog, and fulfilling orders.

1. Introduction

The Supplier Portal connects service providers and product vendors with directory businesses. As a supplier, you can offer products and services that businesses in the directory ecosystem need — from printed menus and marketing materials to IT services and office supplies.

📋 Product Catalog

List your products and services with descriptions, pricing, and images.

📦 Order Management

Receive, review, and fulfill orders from business customers.

🤝 Network

Connect with businesses that need your products or services.

📊 Analytics

Track orders, revenue, and customer engagement over time.

2. Signing Up as a Supplier

Anyone can register as a supplier from the platform homepage or directory pages.

Step-by-Step

1

Go to any directory homepage or the ZaarHub frontend.

2

Click the Register as a Supplier link. It's usually in the footer or the site navigation.

3

The supplier registration form appears. Fill in:

  • Company Name — Your business name
  • Contact Name — Your name or primary contact
  • Email — Used for login and order notifications
  • Phone — Contact number for order inquiries
  • Password — Create a secure password
  • Industry — Select your industry category
  • Description — Brief description of what you offer
4

Click Register. Your account is created and you're redirected to the Supplier Portal.

💡 Tip: Complete your company profile and add products immediately after registering. Businesses searching for suppliers will only see suppliers with a complete profile and at least one product listed.

Supplier Account Verification

New supplier accounts may require admin verification before they appear in search results. You'll receive an email notification once your account is verified. During verification, you can still log in and set up your catalog — your listings just won't be public yet.

4. Managing Product / Service Catalog

Your catalog is how businesses find and order from you. A complete, well-organized catalog attracts more customers.

Adding a Product or Service

1

From the dashboard, click My Catalog and then + Add Product.

2

Fill in the following fields:

Product NameClear, descriptive name for your product or service
CategorySelect from available supplier categories
DescriptionDetailed description, features, and use cases
PriceUnit price or service fee
Unite.g., per item, per hour, per service
ImagesUpload up to 5 product images
AvailabilityIn Stock, Made to Order, or Discontinued
3

Click Save Product. It appears in your catalog immediately.

Managing Existing Products

  • Edit — Click the edit icon on any product to update details, price, or images.
  • Duplicate — Quickly create a similar product by duplicating an existing one.
  • Archive — Remove a product from active listing without deleting it permanently.
  • Delete — Permanently remove a product from your catalog.
  • Bulk actions — Select multiple products to archive or update pricing in bulk.

Catalog Best Practices

  • Clear photos — Use high-quality images that show the product clearly.
  • Detailed descriptions — Include dimensions, materials, use cases, and any relevant specs.
  • Competitive pricing — Research market rates and price competitively.
  • Update regularly — Keep your catalog fresh. Remove discontinued items promptly.
  • Categorize well — Accurate categories help businesses find your products faster.

5. Receiving & Fulfilling Orders

When a business places an order, it appears in your Orders section for processing.

Order Lifecycle

StatusDescription
🆕 NewOrder has been placed but not yet reviewed
🔄 ProcessingYou've accepted the order and are preparing to fulfill
🚚 ShippedOrder has been shipped or service has been delivered
✅ CompletedOrder has been received and confirmed by the customer
❌ CancelledOrder was cancelled (by you or the customer)

Processing an Order

1

Go to Orders in the dashboard. New orders appear at the top.

2

Click on an order to view details: customer info, items ordered, quantity, and any special instructions.

3

Click Accept Order to start processing. The status changes to Processing.

4

Fulfill the order — prepare the product or deliver the service.

5

Click Mark as Shipped once the order is on its way. Add tracking info if available.

6

Order auto-completes when the customer confirms receipt, or you can mark it Complete.

Order Communication

You can message the customer directly from the order detail page:

  • Ask clarifying questions about the order.
  • Provide updates on shipping or availability.
  • Send invoices or additional documentation.

⚠️ Note: If you cannot fulfill an order, click Cancel Order and provide a reason. This notifies the customer so they can find an alternative supplier.

6. Connecting with Business Customers

Building relationships with business customers leads to repeat orders and referrals. The platform provides several ways to connect.

Getting Discovered

  • Complete your profile — A full profile with company details and contact info ranks higher in searches.
  • Add products — Suppliers with more products get more visibility.
  • Respond quickly — Fast response times improve your supplier rating.
  • Collect reviews — Encourage business customers to leave feedback after completed orders.

Customer Profile

When a business places an order, their profile becomes visible in your Customers section. You can:

  • View their business name, contact info, and location.
  • See order history and total spend with you.
  • Send direct messages for repeat business opportunities.
  • Create custom quotes for large or recurring orders.

Best Practices for Suppliers

  • Respond within 24 hours — to inquiries and new orders.
  • Set clear expectations — Display accurate pricing, shipping times, and availability.
  • Quality matters — Deliver quality products and service to earn repeat business.
  • Stay in stock — Update availability regularly. Nothing frustrates customers like out-of-stock items.
  • Communicate proactively — If there's a delay, let the customer know immediately.

Supplier Ratings

Businesses can rate their experience with you after completing an order. Your overall rating is displayed on your supplier profile and influences search ranking. Key rating factors:

  • Product quality — Does the product match the description?
  • Shipping speed — Was the order delivered on time?
  • Communication — Were responses prompt and helpful?
  • Overall satisfaction — Would the customer order again?

💡 Tip: A high supplier rating (4.5+ stars) significantly increases your chances of being selected by businesses. Focus on quality and communication to build a strong reputation.
