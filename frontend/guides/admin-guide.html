🛡️ Admin Guide — Managing the Loyalty Platform

For SwiftSoftware and ZaarHub administrators managing the Program backend.

🏪 1. Business Claim & Verification Flow

When a business owner claims a listing on ZaarHub, the system follows this flow:

Step 1 — Owner Submits Claim

  • Owner goes to /claim-business/ and searches for their business in the directory
  • They must find their business in search results before proceeding — no duplicate listings can be created
  • Step 2: they provide their name, email, phone, and website URL (required, validated format)

Step 2 — Auto-Approval (Instant)

The system checks two things for automatic approval:

  • Email-to-website match — The domain in the owner's email (e.g. @mybusiness.com) is checked against the submitted website domain (e.g. mybusiness.com). If they match → auto-approved
  • Direct email match — If the owner's email exactly matches the business's email field in the directory → auto-approved
  • Submitted website is saved — If the business listing had no website before, the one the claimant submits is saved to the directory automatically

When auto-approved:

  • A visitor account is created with business_type = 'merchant' and a random temp password
  • A business verification record is created with method = 'auto_domain'
  • The claimant sees their temp password on screen and can log in immediately at /portal
  • Their JWT role is set to company_admin — they see the business dashboard
✅ Auto-approval example: Owner with email john@bobsauto.com claims a business and submits website https://bobsauto.com. The domain bobsauto.com matches → instant approval.

Step 3 — Manual Review (Admin Panel)

If auto-approval fails (email domain doesn't match the website), the claim goes to the admin panel for manual review:

  1. Go to https://zaarhub.com/admin/requests.html
  2. Unlock with the admin key
  3. Click the Claims tab → review pending claims
  4. Verify the claimant's identity by checking email, phone, or website
  5. Click Approve or Reject

When manually approved via admin panel:

  • The Node.js API creates a visitor_accounts row with business_type = 'merchant'
  • A temp password is generated and sent via email (requires Mailgun configured)
  • The approval syncs to CoreSwift CRM
⚠️ No website = cannot claim: If a claimant submits without a website URL, the frontend blocks the submission. If a business listing has no website on file and no website is submitted, the claim goes to manual review with a "no website" flag.

What Business Owners See After Approval

  • Login at https://zaarhub.com/portal with their email and temp password
  • Business portal at /business-portal.html with credit rate config, purchase PIN, offers management, scanner, guides
  • They can change their password, manage their listing, create loyalty offers, and track performance

📊 2. Admin Dashboard

The admin panel is your central hub for monitoring and managing the entire ZaarHub Loyalty Program.

  • All tenants — View every merchant, supplier, and participant across the platform
  • All transactions — Monitor credit earnings, redemptions, and referral activity in real-time
  • Flagged activity — Review transactions that triggered automated fraud detection rules
  • System health — Check API status, queue lengths, and service availability
💡 Tip: Pin the dashboard to your browser. It auto-refreshes every 60 seconds so you can spot issues quickly.

👥 3. Tenant Management

As an admin, you have oversight of all tenant accounts. Your responsibilities include:

  • View accounts — Access merchant, supplier, and participant profiles when needed for support
  • Manage configurations — Adjust tenant settings when authorized (credit rates, offer limits, account status)
  • Account recovery — Help merchants and participants regain access to locked accounts
  • Account lifecycle — Activate, suspend, or deactivate accounts according to Program rules
⚠️ Important: Never make configuration changes without documented authorization. All changes should be logged with the requesting party, reason, and approval.

🔍 4. Fraud Monitoring

Fraud detection is one of the most critical admin functions. The system flags suspicious transactions automatically, but you need to investigate.

What to look for:

  • Unusual patterns — A single customer earning credits at many different businesses in a short period, or a business seeing unusual redemption spikes
  • PIN misuse — Multiple verification failures with the same PIN, or verifications occurring at odd hours with no customers present
  • Self-referral loops — Multiple accounts referring themselves using different emails or phone numbers
  • Inflated purchase amounts — A business consistently reporting purchases much higher than their average ticket, especially when the customer is a frequent visitor
  • Collusion indicators — The same customer and business transacting many times in a day with identical amounts
🔎

Review process: Open the flagged transaction → review the customer history and business history → check for matching IPs or account creation dates → make a determination: dismiss flag, warn the party, or escalate for suspension.

🚨 Escalation: If you confirm fraud, immediately suspend the involved accounts and document everything. Notify your supervisor and the security team within 24 hours.

🎧 5. Support & Escalation

Admins handle a variety of support requests from tenants. Common scenarios:

  • Account recovery — Verify identity using email, phone, or security questions, then reset access
  • PIN resets — Generate a new Purchase PIN for merchants who've lost access or suspect compromise
  • Credit corrections — Manually adjust credit balances when system errors occur. Every manual adjustment must include an audit trail: reason, authorization, timestamp
  • Dispute resolution — Mediate between participants and merchants when a transaction is disputed. Review system logs to determine the correct outcome
  • Technical support — Help tenants with portal access, scanner issues, or offer configuration
💡 Tip: For credit corrections, follow the policy: under 1,000 credits = admin-level approval; 1,000+ credits = supervisor authorization required.

📋 6. Audit & Logging

The ZaarHub Platform maintains a comprehensive audit trail. All admin actions are logged and subject to review.

  • All actions logged — Every verification, credit adjustment, account modification, and offer change is recorded with the admin who performed it
  • Manual adjustments documented — When you make a manual credit correction or account change, the system records the before/after state, reason, and your admin ID
  • Regular audit reviews — Audit logs are reviewed on a recurring schedule to ensure compliance with policies
  • Retention — Logs are retained for a minimum of 3 years per regulatory requirements
⚠️ Important: Assume everything you do as an admin is auditable and visible. Never perform actions outside official channels or for personal benefit.

🔒 7. Security Best Practices

Admin accounts have elevated privileges. Follow these practices to keep the platform secure:

  • MFA is required — Multi-factor authentication must be enabled on your admin account. No exceptions
  • No personal account access — Never use admin tools to access your own personal account or accounts of friends/family. If you need customer support, submit a ticket like anyone else
  • Report breaches immediately — If you suspect any security incident (compromised credentials, data exposure, unauthorized access), report it to the security team immediately. Do not investigate on your own
  • Least privilege — Only access the minimum data needed for your current task. Don't browse accounts or transactions out of curiosity
  • Secure your session — Always log out when leaving your workstation. Don't share your admin session with anyone
🚨 Non-compliance: Violations of security policies are grounds for immediate revocation of admin access, termination of employment or contract, and possible legal action.
