Frequently Asked Questions — powered by Offer Palace

What is the Hotel Savings Card?

The Hotel Savings Card is a digital voucher that gives you instant savings on hotel stays at participating properties. It works like a gift card — you receive a code that you can apply toward your hotel booking.

How do I get a Hotel Savings Card?

You can receive a Hotel Savings Card by:

  • Winning it through one of our campaign promotions or spin-to-win games
  • Redeeming loyalty points through your participating business's rewards program
  • Completing an onboarding survey or qualifying action

How much savings can I get?

Hotel Savings Card values range from $100, $200, $300, or $500 depending on the promotion you participated in. The exact amount will be disclosed when you receive your card.

How do I use my Hotel Savings Card?

When you receive your digital card, you'll get a redemption code. Present this code at booking or check-in at a participating hotel to apply your savings toward your stay. Each card has specific terms that will be included with your code.

What hotels are eligible?

Your Hotel Savings Card can be used at participating hotels within our network. The specific hotels available will be listed in your card details. Most major hotel chains and independent properties in our network are included.

When does my card expire?

Each Hotel Savings Card has an expiration date, which will be clearly shown on your card. Most cards are valid for 90 days from issuance. Be sure to book your stay before the expiration date.

Can I use more than one card per booking?

Terms vary by promotion. Some promotions allow stacking multiple cards, while others limit one card per booking. Check the terms provided with your specific card for details.

What if I lose my code?

Contact the business that issued your card, and they can look up your code using your email address or phone number. We recommend saving your code as soon as you receive it.

Can I transfer my card to someone else?

Hotel Savings Cards are generally non-transferable and intended for the recipient only. Exceptions may apply — check the terms provided with your specific card.
